1. Raw Data Cleansing & Normalization

Phase 1

Web-scraped database dumps are notoriously irregular. In this phase, I targeted two datasets: first, a dirty US Presidents list, and second, a raw consumer demographics dataset of 1,000 retail buyers.

Cleansing Protocols Executed:

  • Whitespace Stripping: Used search-and-replace to strip double spaces (e.g. standardizing `Democratic- Republican` to `Democratic-Republican`) and used `TRIM` to remove padding.
  • Abbreviation Expansion: Standardized coding values across all rows to maximize readability in legends (expanded `M`/`S` to `Married`/`Single` and `F`/`M` to `Female`/`Male`).
  • Financial Formatting: Re-cast presidential salary text fields into numeric values and formatted as standard Currency.

2. Demographics Feature Engineering

Phase 2

Individual age values (e.g., 42, 43, 60) are highly fragmented and generate clunky charts. To construct a clear profile of customer demographics, I engineered a dynamic **`Age Bracket`** classification column.

I used a nested logical **`IF`** statement to automatically group consumers into three primary strategic corporate demographics:

=IF(L2 > 54, "Old", IF(L2 >= 31, "Middle Age", IF(L2 < 31, "Adolescent", "Invalid")))

This parses and groups age numbers dynamically:
• **Adolescent:** Age under 31
• **Middle Age:** Age between 31 and 54
• **Old:** Age 55 and above

3. Dynamic BI Dashboard Architecture

Phase 3

To build a highly responsive executive reporting cockpit, I structured the workbook into a modular tab system:

bike_buyers Working Sheet Pivot Table Dashboard

Multi-Dimensional Analysis:

  • Income Splits: Pivot table measuring average earnings of customers who bought bikes vs non-buyers, split by gender (revealing that positive purchases are highly correlated with higher incomes).
  • Commute Utility: Pivot table tracking customer travel distances, revealing that short-commute customers (0-1 miles) purchase bikes at the highest frequency.
  • Demographics Mix: Pivot table aggregating purchases by our newly engineered `Age Brackets` (Adolescent, Middle Age, Old).

Executive Slicers Synchronization:

To give management instantaneous filter controls, I inserted interactive **Slicers (Marital Status, Region, Education)**. By establishing **Report Connections**, a single click on any slicer dynamically filters all three pivot tables and updates all dashboard charts in real-time.

4. Key Demographics Findings

  • Core Consumer Profile: Our highest performing segment comprises **Middle-aged professionals** with an average income above $50,000.
  • Proximity Utility: Commute distance is a massive purchasing factor. Proximity of **0-1 miles** accounts for the largest purchase share; ad spend on audiences commuting more than 5 miles should be heavily minimized.
  • Strategic Ad spend Alignment: Recommend shifting visual ad spend away from Adolescents and Older populations, funneling capital exclusively to middle-aged professional segments.
